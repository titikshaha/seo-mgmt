🎨 Midjourney (Architecture)
AI Image Generation · Concept Rendering · Moodboards
4.2Overall
4.5Visuals
3.8Utility

Midjourney has become an indispensable tool for architects creating concept visuals, client moodboards, and early-stage exterior renders. It cannot generate actual floor plans or technical drawings, but for visual communication and design exploration, nothing matches its output quality. Architects use it to rapidly explore facade styles, material palettes, and building aesthetics before committing to technical design work.

Key Features
Photorealistic renders Facade & exterior design Material exploration Client presentation visuals Rapid concept iteration
Best for: Creating concept visuals and client presentation boards. Not suitable for technical floor plans or construction documentation — use alongside Vitruvi for the full workflow.
03
🤖 Autodesk Forma
AI Early-Stage Design Analysis · Wind, Sun, Noise Simulation
4.1Overall
4.6Analysis
3.2Value

Autodesk Forma (formerly Spacemaker) brings AI-driven environmental analysis to early-stage design — simulating wind flow, sunlight exposure, daylight quality, and noise levels across a site before a single wall is placed. It's a powerful tool for large-scale residential and commercial projects where site performance matters. The downside: it's priced for enterprise and requires a meaningful learning investment.

Key Features
Wind simulation Solar analysis Noise mapping Daylight compliance Revit integration
Best for: Large-scale residential developers and urban planning firms doing site performance analysis. Not suitable for individual house floor plans or small projects.
04
🌿 Spacemaker (Autodesk)
Generative Site Planning · Feasibility Studies
3.9Overall
4.4Planning
2.8Value

Now integrated into Autodesk Forma, Spacemaker's generative design capabilities allow developers to explore hundreds of site layout options against planning constraints, GFA targets, and unit mix requirements — automatically. Excellent for property developers and urban designers, but overkill for anything below large residential or commercial schemes.

Key Features
Generative massing GFA optimisation Planning constraint analysis Unit mix modelling
Best for: Large property developers and urban planners optimising residential site layouts. For individual floor plan generation, Vitruvi is the better fit.
05
📐 TestFit
Rapid Building Feasibility · Massing & Unit Layout
3.8Overall
4.3Speed
3.0Value

TestFit automates building feasibility studies — generating parking layouts, unit configurations, and building massing that fits a given site in minutes. Used primarily by developers and their architects at the very earliest stage of a project to check viability before investing in full design. Strong for multifamily residential and mixed-use schemes in North America.

Key Features
Automated parking layouts Unit configuration Building massing Feasibility reporting
Best for: Real estate developers and architects in North America running feasibility studies on multifamily sites.
06
🔮 Stable Diffusion (Architecture)
Open-source AI Image Generation · Sketch-to-Render
3.5Overall
4.0Flexibility
4.5Cost

Stable Diffusion with architecture-specific ControlNet models allows architects to transform rough sketches or floor plan outlines into photorealistic render concepts. Free and highly customisable, but requires technical setup and prompt engineering skill. Less polished than Midjourney but more controllable — particularly useful for sketch-to-render workflows.

Key Features
Sketch-to-render ControlNet support Open source / free Highly customisable
Best for: Tech-savvy architects and students who want free AI rendering and are comfortable with local installation and prompting.
07
📊 Hypar
Generative BIM · Building Function Automation
3.4Overall
4.1BIM
2.8Ease

Hypar is a generative BIM platform that automates building element generation — from structural grids to MEP routing — through programmable functions. Powerful for BIM-heavy firms wanting to automate repetitive modelling tasks. Steep learning curve and primarily suited for larger architecture and engineering practices with existing BIM workflows.

Key Features
Generative BIM elements Programmable functions IFC export Structural automation
Best for: Large AEC firms with existing BIM infrastructure wanting to automate repetitive modelling tasks.
